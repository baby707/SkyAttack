Piper PA-28

The Piper PA-28 Cherokee is a popular low-wing, single-engine aircraft used by many flight schools for private pilot training. It is known for predictable handling, solid performance, and a stable flight platform.

Aircraft Specifications

  • Engine: Lycoming O-320 or O-360 (varies by model)
  • Seats: 4
  • Cruise Speed: Approximately 110-130 knots
  • Range: Approximately 500 nautical miles
  • Category: Single-engine land aircraft

Training Notes

The Piper PA-28 gives student pilots experience with low-wing aircraft characteristics, including different visibility, fuel system design, and handling compared with high-wing trainers.

Pilot Tips

  • Practice coordinated turns using proper rudder control.
  • Monitor airspeed carefully during approaches.
  • Understand the differences between low-wing and high-wing aircraft.
  • Use checklists consistently before every flight.
